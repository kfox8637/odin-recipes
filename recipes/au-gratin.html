Au-Gratin Potatoes

Creamy and Indulgent Au Gratin Potatoes: A Comfort Food Classic

Au gratin potatoes are a rich and creamy dish that combines thinly sliced potatoes with a luscious cheese sauce, creating a decadent side dish that’s perfect for any occasion. The potatoes are layered in a baking dish and smothered in a velvety sauce made with butter, cream, and a mix of flavorful cheeses like cheddar or Gruyère. Fresh garlic, onions, and a touch of seasoning bring the dish to life, enhancing the natural flavors of the potatoes. As it bakes, the cheese melts and forms a golden, crispy top, while the inside remains soft and tender, creating the perfect balance of textures.

What makes au gratin potatoes so irresistible is the richness of the sauce and the comforting, velvety consistency. The layers of cheese and potatoes meld together, absorbing the flavors as the dish bakes, making each bite more delicious than the last. Whether served alongside a roasted meat or as a standalone dish, au gratin potatoes offer a satisfying, indulgent experience that’s always a hit at dinner tables, holidays, and gatherings. With its combination of creamy, cheesy goodness and crispy golden top, it’s no wonder this dish has remained a favorite for so many years.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium russet potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, sliced into rings
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 ½ cups shredded Cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Butter a 2-quart casserole dish.
  2. Layer 1/2 of the potatoes in the bottom of the prepared casserole dish; season with salt and pepper.
  3. Layer onion slices over top, then top with with remaining potatoes. Season again with salt and pepper.
  4. Melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Gradually whisk in flour and salt and cook, whisking constantly until raw flour flavor has cooked off, about 1 minute.
  5. Gradually add milk, about 1/4 cup at a time, whisking well after each addition to incorporate; the gradual addition and whisking of milk will help avoid lumps in your sauce.
  6. Cook, whisking constantly, until the mixture has thickened, 3 to 5 minutes.
  7. Stir in cheese all at once; continue stirring until melted, 30 to 60 seconds.
  8. Pour cheese sauce over the potatoes, and cover the dish with aluminum foil.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven until potatoes are tender and sauce is bubbly, about 1 ½ hours.
